# 🚀 Data Analysis Project (Real-World Business Case)

## 📌 Overview  
This project was completed during my vacation, where I analyzed my older brother’s small store to understand its sales performance and provide data-driven recommendations for improvement.

## 📅 Project Duration  
June 9 – July 9 (1 Month)

## 📝 Data Collection & Preparation  
I manually recorded daily store transactions with the following fields:  
- Product  
- Price  
- Quantity  
- Units  
- Total Sales  
- Date  

### 🔧 Process  
- Encoded the data in Excel  
- Cleaned the data using Python (Pandas)  
- Loaded the data into Power BI for analysis  

## 📊 Dashboard & Insights  
An interactive dashboard was created with:

- Cards: Quantity Sold, Total Sales, Profit, Unit Sales, Average Sales  
- Bar Chart: Sales per item (top-performing products)  
- Ribbon Chart: Daily sales trend  
- Clustered Column Chart: Most sold items by quantity  

## 📈 Key Findings  
- Total Quantity Sold: 2,000  
- Total Sales: 16,000  
- Profit: 6,000 (after ~9,000+ costs)  
- Sales per Unit: 10.50  
- Average Sales: 17.34  

## 🔥 Top 5 Best-Selling Items (by Sales)  
- Rice – 2,220  
- Mighty White – 1,130  
- Bread – 1,068  
- Mighty Red – 890  
- 4Kanto – 850  

## 📦 Top 5 Items (by Quantity Sold)  
- Bread – 180  
- Mighty White – 148  
- Monay/Filling – 109  
- Small Snacks – 93  
- Mighty Red – 89  

## 📊 Trend Insight  
The daily sales show a stable trend, meaning there is no significant increase or decrease over time.

## 💡 Recommendations  
- Focus on top-selling items  
- Maintain stock of frequently purchased products  
- Reduce slow-moving inventory  

## ✨ Conclusion  
This project demonstrates how data analysis, data cleaning, and visualization can be applied to a real-world business scenario to generate actionable insights.
